# Implementation Plan: TIS-IntelliMat AI-Powered Training Programs
## Partnership between DCEEIC and TIS Holdings Pty Ltd

> **Prepared for**: Department of Cooperative Governance, Economic Affairs, Environment and Traditional Affairs (DCEEIC)
> **Prepared by**: TIS Holdings Pty Ltd
> **Date**: November 2025
> **Status**: Post-MOU Implementation Plan
> **Duration**: 24 months (2-year implementation cycle)

---

## Executive Summary

Following the signed Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) between **DCEEIC** and **TIS Holdings Pty Ltd**, this document provides the detailed implementation plan for deploying the **TIS-IntelliMat AI-Powered Training Programs** across multiple levels:

1. **Community Level** - Smallholder farmers, cooperatives, rural communities
2. **Staff Level** - Government extension officers, cooperative administrators
3. **Management Level** - Department heads, municipal officials, program managers
4. **Enterprise Level** - SMME owners, cooperative leaders, agribusiness entrepreneurs

### Implementation Overview

**Total Beneficiaries (24 months)**: 5,000+ individuals across Limpopo Province

**Training Levels**:
- 3,000 community members (farmers, youth, women)
- 1,500 staff members (extension officers, administrators)
- 300 management personnel (department heads, municipal officials)
- 200 enterprise leaders (SMME owners, cooperative leaders)

**Budget**: R12.5 million over 24 months (detailed breakdown provided)

**Partners**: TIS Holdings, UNIVEN, Agricultural Research Council, Local Municipalities, Cooperatives

**Joint Steering Committee (JSC)**:
- **Meetings**: Quarterly (4 times per year)
- **Mandate**: Strategic direction, budget approval, dispute resolution, policy alignment
- **Members**: 8-10 senior leaders

**Project Management Committee (PMC)**:
- **Meetings**: Monthly
- **Mandate**: Operational oversight, progress monitoring, resource allocation
- **Members**: 6-8 program managers

**Technical Working Group (TWG)**:
- **Meetings**: Weekly (during implementation phases)
- **Mandate**: Technical implementation, problem-solving, quality assurance
- **Members**: 10-15 technical staff

### 1.3 Communication Protocol

**Reporting Structure**:
- **Weekly**: Technical updates (TWG to PMC)
- **Monthly**: Progress reports (PMC to JSC)
- **Quarterly**: Impact reports (JSC to DCEEIC leadership)
- **Annually**: Comprehensive evaluation report

**Escalation Path**:
1. Technical issues → TWG → PMC (2 days)
2. Operational issues → PMC → JSC (1 week)
3. Strategic/policy issues → JSC → DCEEIC HOD (immediate)

## 2. Training Programs by Level

### LEVEL 1: Community Training Programs

#### Program 1A: Sustainable Farmer Training (Core Program)

**Target Audience**: Smallholder farmers (subsistence and emerging)

**Beneficiaries**: 2,000 farmers over 24 months

**Duration**: 6 months per cohort (4 cohorts)

**Eligibility**:
- Active farmers (land ownership or access)
- Registered with local cooperative or agricultural office
- Basic literacy (or accompanied by literate family member)
- Commitment to attend 80% of sessions

**Curriculum** (100 hours total):

| Module | Duration | Topics | Delivery |
|--------|----------|--------|----------|
| **M1: Sustainable Agriculture Basics** | 2 weeks | Principles, benefits, success stories | WhatsApp bot + workshop |
| **M2: Soil Health Management** | 3 weeks | Soil testing, composting, fertilizers | Hands-on + AI advisor |
| **M3: Water Conservation** | 3 weeks | Irrigation, rainwater harvesting, drought management | Practical demos + app |
| **M4: Climate-Smart Practices** | 4 weeks | Weather forecasting, crop selection, adaptation | AI coach + field visits |
| **M5: Integrated Pest Management** | 3 weeks | Natural pesticides, beneficial insects, disease prevention | Visual guides + AI |
| **M6: Market Access & Cooperatives** | 2 weeks | Pricing, buyers, cooperative benefits | Market visits + webinar |
| **M7: Record Keeping & Finance** | 2 weeks | Farm records, budgeting, loan applications | Mobile app training |
| **Assessment & Certification** | 1 week | Practical assessment, certificate ceremony | On-farm evaluation |

**Delivery Methods**:
- **WhatsApp Learning Bot**: Daily tips, Q&A in Tshivenda/Xitsonga
- **Monthly Workshops**: Full-day sessions at community centers (8 sessions)
- **Peer Learning Circles**: Weekly meetings of 10-15 farmers
- **On-Farm Visits**: 2 visits per farmer by extension officers
- **AI Tutor Support**: 24/7 via mobile app (voice and text)

**Certification**: TIS-IntelliMat Certified Sustainable Farmer + DCEEIC Recognition

**Expected Outcomes**:
- 80% completion rate
- +25% average crop yield improvement
- +20% income increase
- -25% water usage reduction
- 70% adopt at least 3 sustainable practices

## 8. Risk Management & Mitigation

### 8.1 Risk Register

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation Strategy |
|------|------------|--------|---------------------|
| **Low internet connectivity in rural areas** | High | High | • Offline mode for mobile app<br>• WhatsApp bot (low bandwidth)<br>• SMS fallback<br>• Physical learning centers |
| **Low digital literacy among farmers** | High | Medium | • Voice-based learning<br>• Peer learning circles<br>• Facilitator support<br>• Family involvement |
| **Language barriers** | Medium | High | • Full Tshivenda/Xitsonga localization<br>• Bilingual facilitators<br>• Visual learning aids |
| **Participant attrition** | Medium | Medium | • Certificates and incentives<br>• Peer support groups<br>• Flexible scheduling<br>• Transport support |
| **Budget constraints** | Medium | High | • Phased implementation<br>• In-kind contributions<br>• Private sector sponsorships |
| **Platform technical failures** | Low | High | • 99.9% uptime SLA<br>• 24/7 support<br>• Redundancy and backups<br>• Disaster recovery plan |
| **Limited farmer participation** | Medium | High | • Community leaders endorsement<br>• Success stories and testimonials<br>• Tangible benefits (yield, income)<br>• Financial incentives (subsidies, grants) |
| **Staff resistance to technology** | Medium | Medium | • Change management strategy<br>• Hands-on training<br>• Champion identification<br>• Management support |
| **Delays in partner coordination** | Medium | Medium | • Clear roles and SLAs<br>• Regular TWG meetings<br>• Escalation protocols<br>• Buffer time in schedules |
| **Political/policy changes** | Low | High | • JSC oversight and buy-in<br>• Document commitments (MOU)<br>• Broad stakeholder support<br>• Quick wins and visibility |

### 8.2 Contingency Plans

**If enrollment is low**:
- Increase marketing and community mobilization
- Offer incentives (transport, refreshments)
- Partner with cooperatives for batch enrollment
- Extend enrollment period

**If completion rates are low**:
- Identify barriers through surveys
- Adjust course difficulty/length
- Increase facilitator support
- Offer catch-up sessions

**If technology fails**:
- Activate backup systems immediately
- Switch to manual/paper-based temporarily
- Compensate with extra facilitator-led sessions
- Escalate to TIS Holdings technical team

**If budget is insufficient**:
- Prioritize critical activities
- Seek additional funding (donors, private sector)
- Reduce non-essential costs
- Extend timeline to spread costs
